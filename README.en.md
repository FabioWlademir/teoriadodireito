## 🧠 About the Research Group

The group investigates the transformations occurring in Law stemming from the structural crises of the Contemporary State and the impacts of globalization. Using a transdisciplinary approach grounded in complexity, the studies combine **Social Systems Theory** (Niklas Luhmann) and **Legal Criticism** (Luis Alberto Warat).

### 📌 Active Research Lines:

1. **Society, New Rights, and Transnationalization:** Reflections on emerging rights (Bioethics, Environment, Intellectual Property, and Fundamental Human Rights) from a complexity perspective.
2. **Intersystemic Constitutionalism:** Analysis of the insufficiency of traditional Constitutional Theory when facing new legal-constitutional problems raised to the global society level.
3. **COFECUB Project (Brazil / France):** International cooperation studying the Constitutional State and states of emergency resulting from multisectoral crises and syndemics.
